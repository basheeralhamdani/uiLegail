LADY BIRD DEED

Grantee Information (Person receiving the property)
- Full Legal Name: Complete name as it should appear on legal documents
- Complete Mailing Address: Street address, city, state, ZIP code

Grantor Information (Current property owner transferring the property)
- Full Legal Name: Complete name as it appears on current property deed
- Complete Mailing Address: Street address, city, state, ZIP code

Property Details (Complete legal description of the real estate)
- Lot Number: Official lot designation from plat map
- Subdivision Name: Official recorded subdivision name
- County: County where property is located
- Legal Description: Complete legal description from current deed or survey
- Tax Parcel Number: Official tax assessor's parcel identification number
- Common Street Address: Physical address of the property (e.g., 123 Main Street, City, State ZIP)

Conveyance Rights & Powers (Legal rights retained by grantor)
- Lifetime Rights: Grantor retains full ownership and control during their lifetime (life estate)
- Power to Convey: Grantor retains right to sell, mortgage, or transfer property without grantee consent

Tax Exemptions & Legal Clauses
- Transfer Tax Exemption: Documentation that transfer qualifies for tax exemption under applicable state law

Execution & Notarization Details
- Execution Date: Date when deed is signed and executed
- Notary Public Name: Full name of notary public witnessing signatures
- Notary Commission Expiration: Date when notary's commission expires
- Law Firm Details: Attorney/firm name, address, contact information for document preparation


FULL TRUST

Grantor Information (Person creating and funding the trust)
- Full Legal Name: Complete name of the grantor as it appears on legal documents
- Complete Mailing Address: Street address, city, state, ZIP code (e.g., Dearborn, Michigan)
- Date of Trust Execution: Date when the trust is signed (e.g., July 31, 2025)

Trust Identification (Details to uniquely identify the trust)
- Trust Name: Formal name of the revocable living trust (e.g., [Grantor's Name] Revocable Living Trust)
- Amendments: Any prior amendments to the trust, if applicable

Family Information (Details about marital status and descendants for distribution purposes)
- Marital Status: Whether unmarried, married, widowed, or divorced
- Children's Names and Birthdates: Full names and dates of birth for each child
- References to Descendants: Specification that descendants include children and their descendants, including any deceased

Trustee Information (Persons managing the trust)
- Initial Trustee Name: Name of the initial trustee (typically the grantor)
- Successor Trustee Names: Names of successor trustees in order (e.g., First successor, then second if first unable)
- Co-Trustee Names: Any co-trustees if applicable

Beneficiary Information (Persons or entities receiving trust assets)
- Beneficiary Names: Full names of primary beneficiaries (e.g., children or others)
- Shares or Percentages: Distribution shares for each beneficiary (e.g., 50% to each child)
- Contingent Beneficiaries: Names and shares if primary beneficiaries predecease or are unable
- Special Provisions: Any specific conditions, such as for underage or incapacitated beneficiaries

Asset Details (Property transferred into the trust, listed in Schedule A)
- Asset Descriptions: Complete list of assets, including real estate (legal description, address, parcel number), personal property, bank accounts, investments, and other tangible/intangible items
- Initial Funding: Nominal initial asset (e.g., Ten Dollars cash) to establish the trust

Powers and Provisions (Specific rights retained or granted)
- Reserved Powers: Grantor's retained powers, such as to amend, revoke, add/remove property, or control distributions
- Incapacity Definitions: Criteria for determining grantor's incapacity (e.g., opinion of two physicians or court determination)

Tax and Legal Clauses (Compliance and exemptions)
- Tax Identification Number: Social Security Number of grantor used as trust's tax ID
- Governing Law: State laws under which the trust is governed (e.g., Michigan)

Execution & Notarization Details (Formal signing requirements)
- Execution Date: Date when trust is signed
- Notary Public Name: Full name of notary witnessing signature
- Notary Commission Expiration: Date when notary's commission expires
- Witness Names: Names of any required witnesses, if applicable (though not always needed for trusts in Michigan)
